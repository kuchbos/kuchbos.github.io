Oleg Sobchuk: Macroevolution of the Arts
About Publications Research

What I study

I study long-term patterns in the history of various arts – literature, film, or music – using large datasets. Imporatntly, I also explain these patterns – using scientific theories, often borrowed from ecology, evolution, psychology, or economics. Namely, I am interested in how and why various artistic forms get invented, how they succeed or fail, and how they get transmitted between generations of artists.

Who I am

I studied in Kyiv (Ukraine), Tartu (Estonia), and Stanford (USA). Since 2018 I have been researching the evolution of arts at the Max Planck Society: first, alongside Olivier Morin at the MPI for the Science of Human History (Jena, Germany), and later with Bret Beheim at the MPI for Evolutionary Anthropology (Leipzig, Germany).
