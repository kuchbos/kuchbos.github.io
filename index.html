Oleg Sobchuk: Macroevolution of the Arts
About Publications Research

What I study

I am a researcher studying “macroevolution of the arts.” What does it mean? I discover long-term patterns in the history of various arts – literature, film, or music – using large datasets. But not only that: I also explain these patterns – using scientific theories, often borrowed from ecology, evolution, or economics. For example, I am explaining the staggering complexity of modern cinema, the explosive evolution of early literary fiction, and the blinding fame of music stars. All of these are end products of the hidden “machinery” of culture. I study the cogs of this machinery.

Who I am

I come from a small, pretty town in western Ukraine – Kremenets. I did my bachelor’s in literature at Kyiv-Mohyla Academy, after which went on to study at the University of Tartu, in Estonia, where I defended my PhD thesis Charting Artistic Evolution (supervised by Franco Moretti and Arne Merilai): an attempt to create a Frankenstein out of art history, computational methods, and the theory of cultural evolution. Afterwards, I kept digging in the same direction – macroevolution of the arts – first, alongside Olivier Morin at the Max Planck Institute for the Science of Human History (Jena, Germany), and later with Bret Beheim at the Max Planck Institute of Evolutionary Anthropology (Leipzig, Germany). Recently, my digging was rewarded with actual gold: a grant from the Cultural Evolution Society.
